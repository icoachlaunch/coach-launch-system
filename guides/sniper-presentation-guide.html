What the Sniper Presentation™ Is — and What It Isn't

The Sniper Presentation™ is your live event. It's the masterclass you run — on Zoom, on a stage, in a room — where you deliver, you move people, and you make your offer. It ends with the Sniper Close™: the closing sequence that turns an audience into clients.

Here's the part that changes everything. You've already written this. Your SCORE™ Card is the master copy — five acts, your whole story and system and offer, locked. The Enrollment Doc took that card and made it a written sales letter. The Sniper Presentation™ takes the same card and makes it a spoken pitch for a live room.

So this tool isn't writing your message. It's arranging it — and adding the live layer.

The live layer is three things: engagement (a room that answers, types, and raises hands), story bits (the timeless stories that raise emotion at the right moment), and the close (the exact beats that ask for the sale without being weird).

What it is NOT:

It's not a lecture. A lecture teaches everything and sells nothing. You're not here to dump information — you're here to move people to a decision.

It's not a "webinar" in the tired sense — slides droning at a silent audience. It's a live, interactive event where the room is part of the show.

It's not a separate pitch bolted on the end. The whole presentation is the pitch. Every act sets up the close. By the time you make the offer, it's the obvious next step, not a surprise.

How You'll Build It

Two decisions first, then four phases.

The two decisions set everything else: your format (a single-day Masterclass or a 3-day Challenge) and your close (Enrollment, Strategy, or Deposit). Both come straight off your Money Model's phase — you're mostly confirming, not guessing.

Then you build the presentation one phase at a time — WHO → WHAT → WHY → WHEN — as slide beats plus the words you actually say, with a story bit dropped into every act. You never build it out of order, because the order is the engine.

The Two Formats — Masterclass or Challenge

Same spine, two lengths. Which one you run depends on your phase.

Masterclass — a single day.

One event. It runs tighter early on (hook, story, system, offer, close) and gets longer as you grow — more proof, more teaching, more story bits. The acts don't change; the depth does.

Challenge — a 3-day event.

The exact same spine, split across three days so the immersion runs deeper: Day 1 = Phase 1 (WHO) and belief-building; Day 2 = Phase 2 (WHAT), the system; Day 3 = Phases 3–4 (WHY + WHEN), the offer and the close. You fill the extra time with more stories and deeper teaching — never with new acts.

The rule that governs both:

The deeper the immersion, the higher the conversion — and the shorter the sales cycle. A Challenge closes harder than a Masterclass because people go further with you before you ever ask. You earn your way to the longer formats; you don't start there.

The Four Phases, Up Close

Here's what each phase is really doing — and how to know you've got it right.

Phase 1 · WHO — Open Loop

Earn their trust and open the loop

What it is: the opening. Two SCORE™ acts live here — Serendipity (the hook) and Connection (your story). You hook the room with your $Million Promise™, name exactly who this is for, introduce the enemy and position yourself as the guide, mirror their pain, and tell your $Million Moment™ — the discovery story. You also open a loop (a gift at the end) that keeps them to the close.

Why it matters: people buy from people they trust. If this phase lands, the room leans in for everything after it. If it doesn't, nothing downstream can save the close.

✓ Right: "You've got the expertise — but when someone asks what you do, you fumble a 3-minute answer that loses them." (They nod.) ✗ Wrong: "Today we'll cover several strategies to grow your business." (Nobody leans in.)

Phase 2 · WHAT — Token

Reveal the system and make them want it

What it is: the Opportunity act. You deliver the Big Shift (the one reframe), reveal your Magic Formula™ (the 3 stages, 9 steps), contrast Hell-without vs Heaven-with, introduce the Two Identities, show proof, and run a Confidence Check. You seed the Token — the gift that ties to the offer.

Why it matters: this is where desire is built. You show them enough that they think "I need this — and I need you to walk me through it." You reveal the what and the why; you don't teach every how. That's what the offer is for.

✓ Right: walk all 9 steps as outcomes — what each one gets them. ✗ Wrong: teach one step so deeply the room feels "done" and never wants the rest.

Phase 3 · WHY — the Sniper Close™

Bust the beliefs, then make the offer

What it is: the Remove Risk act flowing straight into the offer. First you dissolve the internal beliefs holding them back (belief → bust-it), show why the old models fail, and prove your system solves each. Then the Pitch Primer asks permission, and you make the offer: value stack, anchor, bridge, price — and the close. This is the heart of the whole event.

Why it matters: you earned the pitch by delivering real value. Making the offer clearly is a service — it gives people the chance to get more of what they just felt. Soft-pedal it and you rob them of the decision.

✓ Right: "You've seen the what and the why. Let me show you the how — together." Then the offer, owned and clear. ✗ Wrong: "Sorry, here's the salesy bit…" — apologizing for the pitch you earned.

Phase 4 · WHEN — Re-Pitch

Close the fence-sitters

What it is: the Execution act. You seed and handle the biggest external objections (time, money, "tried before," "my niche's different"), paint the Fork in the Road (two futures), give real urgency, state the guarantee if you have one, re-pitch with a clear CTA cadence, run Q&A, and send them off on a high with a Story-Jam close.

Why it matters: most of the room won't decide on the first ask. The Fork in the Road and the objection handling move more people than any discount ever will. This is the phase people are waiting for — give it to them.

✓ Right: "Here's where you'll be in 90 days if you don't — and here's where you'll be if you do." ✗ Wrong: say the price once and just… wait.

Story Bits — the Emotional Engine

Story is the driver. Facts inform; stories move. And it's emotion — not logic — that makes the sale. So every act gets a speaker bit: a timeless, repeatable story that does one job at one moment.

Every bit follows the same six-part shape:

Hook (an engagement setup — "how many of you…?") → Story (the metaphor, with call-and-response baked in) → Turn (bridge it to their life) → Target (the belief or objection it breaks) → Soundbyte (the line they repeat) → Transition (into the next beat or the close).

Where they go:

Pre-pitch bits — engagement, hype, and empowering-belief stories — live in Phases 1–2. They warm the room and open minds. Re-pitch bits — belief-break, objection-break, and close-prime stories — live in Phases 3–4. They fill your framework's "Belief / Bust-It" and "Objection / Handle-It" rows and drive the decision.

You've got a whole bit library to pull from, sorted by job and by the exact belief or objection each one breaks — plus a template to build your own. Length flexes on bits: a Masterclass runs a lean set, a Challenge adds more into the same acts. Story is what stretches the event without breaking the structure.

One honest rule about stories:

Any story you tell as your own has to be true and yours. Borrowed teaching stories — the monkey trap, the eagle, the crab bucket — are fair game: retell them in your words and credit where they came from. Never pass someone else's life off as yours. A live room can feel a fake, and it costs you everything you built.

The VIP Hour (Growth and Mastery only)

Once you're at Growth or Mastery, you've got a VIP tier — so you can run a VIP Hour: a bonus session just for VIP ticket holders where you over-deliver, build deeper rapport, and reveal a big secret. It's what makes the VIP worth paying for. (The VIP itself was built in your Cash Flow Max™.)

Placement is a choice — and it matters.

At the start is the fuller experience: it warms your VIPs up and they carry that energy into the main room. Best when you expect a solid VIP turnout live.

At the end is the smart fallback: if live attendance is thin, a pre-event VIP hour can feel flat. Running it after the main event keeps the room full and rewards the VIPs who stay. Below Growth, there's no VIP tier yet — so there's no VIP Hour. Skip it.

Hard Rules

Arrange, don't re-write. Every beat pulls from your locked assets. This tool doesn't re-collect your message — it stages it.

Never skip or reorder an act. The SCORE™ arc is the conversion engine. Longer events add depth and story; they never change the order.

One of three closes — never a fourth. Enrollment, Strategy, or Deposit, matched to your Money Model.

A story in every act. No dead acts. If an act has no bit, it's a lecture slide, not a moment.

Spoken, not written. Short sentences, direct address, built-in engagement. Never read your sales letter at the room.

Only your real numbers and true stories. No invented results, prices, or personal stories. Borrowed parables get credited.

Common Mistakes That Kill the Close

Teaching instead of moving

The most common killer. You know your stuff, so you teach all of it — and the room leaves "satisfied" with no reason to buy. A full brain doesn't open a wallet; a moved heart does.

The fix: reveal the what and the why, save the how for the offer. Cut any teaching that doesn't push toward the close, and put a story where the teaching was.

A quiet room

No hand-raises, no chat, no call-and-response. A silent audience is a dead audience — they've drifted, and drifted people don't buy.

The fix: open with the train-the-room bit to set the "you do this, you don't watch this" contract, then keep asking for a YES, a fill-in-the-blank, a hand. Engagement is the conversion primer.

Apologizing for the pitch

"Sorry, here comes the salesy part." The second you apologize, you tell the room the offer is something to be ashamed of — and they believe you.

The fix: own the transition. You earned the pitch by delivering real value. "Let me show you how we do this together" — no apology, no flinch.

Skipping the Fork in the Road

You make the offer, state the price, and wait. Most of the room needs to see both futures before they move — and without the Fork, they default to "I'll think about it," which means no.

The fix: paint it honestly — where they'll be in 90 days if they don't act, and if they do. It moves more people than any discount.

Wrong length for the phase

Running a 3-day Challenge when you're still validating in Launch — or cramming a Mastery-level close into a 45-minute Masterclass. The format has to match the phase or the math doesn't hold.

The fix: take the format your Money Model's phase points to. Earn your way up to the longer, deeper formats.

Borrowing a story as your own

Telling a famous speaker's personal story as if it happened to you. It feels powerful right up until someone in the room has heard it before — then your whole credibility evaporates.

The fix: tell your own true stories in the personal slots; use the library's parables as parables, credited. Truth is what makes a live room trust you.
