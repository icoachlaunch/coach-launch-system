What This Is (and Why It Matters)

The Presentation Engine™ takes something you've already built — your Sniper Presentation™ Script, a masterclass outline, a training — and turns it into a full slide deck. On-brand. In minutes. Built by AI, presented from your own laptop.

Here's the shift. Most people treat slides as a design project. You open PowerPoint, fight with templates, drag boxes around for hours, and end up with a deck you use once. Next launch, you start over from a blank file. Nothing carries forward.

You don't have a slide problem. You have a template problem.

Slides are just HTML. And once they're HTML, you build them once and reuse them forever. Your cover, your bio, your value stack, your close — build each once, save it, and the AI assembles any new deck from your asset. Your slide work compounds for the first time.

✦ The one that makes this special

Your Sniper Presentation™ Script from Step 4 already comes as slides in disguise. Every beat has a SLIDE line (what's on screen) and a SPEAKER SCRIPT line (what you say). The Presentation Engine™ reads that script and turns each beat into a real slide — and it keeps your speaker script as private presenter notes only you see. You're not designing from scratch. You're converting a script that's already in slide form.

This tool isn't one of the nine Formula steps. It's a production tool — it takes what the Formula built and turns it into the thing you stand up and deliver. It lives closest to the Money Magnet™ and pairs with Step 4, The Sniper Close™.

What's in the Kit

The Presentation Engine™ has two pieces you keep, plus two things you bring.

1

The Presentation Engine™ Builder

The "brain." It's the prompt that teaches your AI exactly how to turn your asset into on-brand slides — picking the right layout for each section, using your colors and fonts, writing each slide as clean HTML, and assembling the deck. You install it once.

2

The Slide Viewer

One HTML file (slide-viewer.html) — your presentation player. The Builder writes your slides straight into it. You double-click it to present: arrow keys to move, F for fullscreen, a menu for all slides. No server, no database, no internet once it's built.

And two things you bring:

A

A finished asset

Best of all, your Sniper Presentation™ Script (Step 4). Or another completed asset — a Masterclass or Challenge outline, a client training, a talk. Don't have one yet? The Builder points you to the tool that makes it first.

B

Your brand

Your Business Brand Profile + Visual Style Guide (your Brand Foundations). This is what makes the deck yours instead of generic — your colors, your fonts, your logo, your voice.

The whole packet is small on purpose. A builder, a viewer, your asset, your brand. That's it. No complicated folder of files to manage.

The Big Upgrade: A Real Presenter View

Most slide tools make you choose: put your talk track ON the slide (so the room reads it and tunes you out), or keep it somewhere else and lose your place live. The Presentation Engine™ solves both, because your Sniper Presentation™ Script already separates the two.

✓ On the slide (audience)

One idea. A headline, a visual anchor, minimal words — in your brand. The room looks at it for a second and looks back at you.

✗ Not on the slide

Your full talk track. A slide crammed with everything you plan to say is a teleprompter, not a slide — and the room reads ahead instead of listening.

So your SPEAKER SCRIPT rides along as presenter notes — hidden from the audience, there for you. In the Slide Viewer you get:

Present

Pop-out Presenter View

Press P to open a second window for your laptop screen: your speaker script big, the next slide previewed, a timer, the phase and act — while the room sees only the clean slide on the projector.

Notes

On-screen notes

Press S to slide your speaker notes up on this screen — perfect for rehearsal, or when you're presenting from one screen and want your cues.

Timer

Run sheet + timer

Press T to start a timer, and open the ☰ run sheet to see every slide grouped by phase — so you pace the room and never lose the thread.

✔ Why this matters for the close

A Sniper Presentation™ builds trust, then desire, then belief, then the decision. That only works if the room is watching YOU, not reading your script off the wall. Clean slides for them, full script for you — that's how the room closes itself.

Setup — Choose Your Tool (~10 Minutes)

Setup is a one-time thing. Pick the tool you use most and follow those steps. After this, building decks is just a conversation.

Cowork or Claude Code (recommended)

This is the best setup — your AI can read your files, write the slides, and (if you want) generate images, all in one project.

Step 1

Put the Slide Viewer in your project

Download slide-viewer.html from Coach Launch Academy™ and drop it in your project folder — the same place your Brand Foundations live. That's the file you'll present from.

Step 2

Install The Presentation Engine™ Builder

Keep the builder prompt in your project (or install it as a skill your AI loads automatically). Then just say what you want built.

Step 3

(Optional) Turn on images

Only if you want AI-generated images on a few slides. Use the project's kie-image-gen skill — it handles your Kie.ai key once and downloads the pictures for you. Skip this if you're going all-HTML. (See "Adding Images" below.)

Step 4

Start building

Open a chat in your project and say: "Build my slides from my Sniper Presentation™ Script." The Builder takes it from there.

Claude or ChatGPT Projects (web)

No project folder? Run the Builder as a saved project on the web. It works the same — it just can't auto-generate images (you'll add those by hand; see below).

Step 1

Create a project

Make a new Project (or Custom GPT). Name it "Presentation Engine" or "[Your Business] Slides."

Step 2

Paste the Builder as your instructions

Open the builder prompt, select all, copy it, and paste it into the project's custom instructions. Now the AI always knows how to build your slides.

Step 3

Add the Viewer + your brand

Upload slide-viewer.html and your Brand Foundations (Business Brand Profile, Visual Style Guide) so the AI can use them.

Step 4

Start building

Open a chat and say: "Build my slides from my Sniper Presentation™ Script."

✦ Just want to try it once?

You don't even need to install anything — paste the whole builder prompt into any chat with your asset and say "build my slides." Installing it is just so you never have to paste it again.

Build Your First Deck (~20–30 Minutes)

Once you're set up, building a deck is a guided conversation. Here's exactly how it goes.

1

Have your asset ready

Best case: your finished Sniper Presentation™ Script. Don't have one? The Builder points you to The Sniper Close™ Builder (Step 4) — or the matching builder for another asset — then you come back. Building the asset first is the rule that makes everything else work.

2

Point it at your brand

The Builder reads your Business Brand Profile + Visual Style Guide — colors, fonts, logo, voice — and maps them onto every slide. This is what makes the deck look like yours.

3

Say "build my slides"

It reads your asset and brand, then shows you a quick plan — which slide type each section becomes, and which slides could use an image. You approve the plan or tweak it.

4

Choose: walk through, or build it all

Walk-through if you want to confirm each slide as it's built; as-is if you trust the asset and just want the deck. One slide at a time, or straight through.

5

Preview in the Viewer

It writes your slides into slide-viewer.html. Double-click it, read through the deck. Arrow keys to move, F for fullscreen, P to check your presenter view.

6

Refine in plain English

Tell the AI what to fix the way you'd tell a person: "tighten slide 6," "make slide 4 a comparison," "make that story slide darker," "swap the accent." No design software — you edit with words.

7

Present

Save the file and present from your browser, full-screen, with your notes on your own screen. It's yours — share the file, host it, or keep it. No license, no lock-in, no expiring export.

How Each Beat Becomes a Slide

The Builder doesn't guess. It maps each part of your asset to a proven slide type. For a Sniper Presentation™, the map is nearly one-to-one with your Script's beats.

Beat in your Sniper ScriptBecomes this slideImage?

Who this is forQualify — "for you / not for you"No
The $Million Moment™ storyStory-bit — styled as a change of paceMood

The offer — value stack → priceOffer / value-stackNo
Proof / resultsProof — real testimonials onlyNo
The Close (Enrollment / Strategy / Deposit)CTA — the single next moveYes

Story-bit slides get a distinct look on purpose — a darker background, a full-bleed mood image, one line of text — so a story feels like a change of pace and the room leans in. The story itself lives in your presenter notes, not on the slide.

One idea per slide

If a section holds three ideas, it becomes three slides — not one busy slide with three columns of text. Fewer, richer slides beat more, thinner ones. The slide is a visual anchor for the point you're making out loud.

Making It Look Like Yours (Auto-Brand)

You don't answer twenty questions about colors and fonts. The Builder reads your Brand Foundations and maps them straight onto the slides.

From your Visual Style Guide: your hero color and neutrals, your two fonts and size feel, your logo and wordmark rules, your imagery mood. From your Business Brand Profile: your presenter name and brand name (the mark in the corner of every slide), your positioning and personality (the mood of any images), and your voice (how the on-slide copy reads).

It echoes back what it pulled and asks you to confirm before it builds. If something's missing, it asks once — and if it still can't find it, it uses a clean default and tells you it did. You're never guessing what your deck will look like.

✦ Keep your Brand Foundations current

The more your Business Brand Profile and Visual Style Guide know your real colors, fonts, and voice, the more every deck looks like you and not a template. Update those docs and every future deck upgrades with them.

Adding Images (Optional)

Most teaching, framework, and data slides look best as clean HTML — no image needed. But a few slides pop with one: your cover, section dividers, problem slides, story-bits, and your closing CTA. Images are optional. You turn them on only if you want them.

The rule is hybrid: the text stays in HTML — crisp, on-brand, perfectly spelled — and the AI generates only the picture behind it. Never let AI put words inside an image; it misspells and goes off-brand. Text in HTML, picture from AI.

In Cowork or Claude Code, use the project's kie-image-gen skill. It asks for your Kie.ai key once, lets you preview and approve, and downloads the picture for you. On the web, generate the image in Kie.ai's web app and tell the Builder which one to use on each slide — same hybrid result, a couple more clicks.

✔ Cost is tiny

Images run about ten cents each through your own Kie.ai account. A whole deck is usually a dollar or two — and you only image-up the few slides that earn it.

Build Once, Reuse Forever

This is where slides stop being a chore. Ask the Builder to save the slides you'll reuse on every deck — your cover, your bio / credibility, your value stack, and your CTA.

Each one gets saved as a small labelled block. On your next deck, the Builder pulls the template, swaps the words, and moves on. Your first deck takes about half an hour. Every deck after that takes minutes — because these carry forward.

Tips for Great Decks

Asset first, always. The deck is only as good as the asset. Get your Sniper Presentation™ Script tight in Step 4 before you build slides. The script is the spec; slides just visualize it.

Feed it your real Brand Foundations. The more it knows your colors, fonts, and voice, the more the deck looks like you and not a template.

Fewer, richer slides beat more, thinner ones. Let each slide carry one real idea, and talk through it. The teaching is in your mouth, not crammed on the wall.

Use your presenter view. Rehearse with S; present with P on a second screen. Your script is right there — you never have to memorise or read off the wall.

Save your templates. Cover, bio, value stack, CTA. Build them once and every future deck pulls from them.

Common Mistakes

✗ Don't

Try to write your Sniper Presentation™ inside this tool. It builds slides — it doesn't write the pitch.

✓ Do

Build your Script first with The Sniper Close™ Builder (Step 4), then bring the finished Script here.

✗ Don't

Expect it to invent testimonials, stats, or results to fill a proof slide. It won't make those up — and you wouldn't want it to.

✓ Do

Give it your real proof. No results yet? Tell it — it uses your own story as the proof point instead.

✗ Don't

Dump your whole talk track onto the slide so the room reads ahead of you.

✓ Do

Keep the slide to one idea and let your speaker script live in the presenter notes, where only you see it.

✗ Don't

Treat every deck as a from-scratch design project — the PowerPoint habit.

✓ Do

Save your templates once and reuse them. The second deck takes a fraction of the time.

Heads up — keep it one file

The single-file Slide Viewer opens with a double-click and works offline. If you ever split slides into a separate folder, a plain double-click can hit browser security — so stick with the single file, or preview it inside your AI tool. For almost everyone, the one file is the answer.
