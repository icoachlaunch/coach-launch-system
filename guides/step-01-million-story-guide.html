Do This — Not That

The difference between a message that stops the scroll and one that gets ignored is almost always specificity.

✓ Do this

Name one person. Name one outcome. Name the timeframe. Talk to them — "maybe you've felt this too..."

✕ Not that

"I help entrepreneurs unlock their potential and scale to the next level." Nobody feels read by that.

"Free attracts tire-kickers. Paid attracts buyers." Specific attracts the right person. Vague attracts nobody.

Common Mistakes & Fixes

⚠ Talking to everyone

If it's for everyone, it lands on no one. Fix: pick the ONE person you help best and write only to them.

⚠ Leading with your credentials

Nobody cares yet. Fix: lead with their pain first — the thing that keeps them up at night.

⚠ Vague outcomes

"Grow your business" says nothing. Fix: use a real number — the more specific, the more believable.

Score Your Own

Read your message out loud. Then check it against these.

  • It names ONE specific person — not "everyone".
  • It states a concrete outcome, with a number where possible.
  • It names the painful alternative they get to avoid.
  • A stranger in your audience would feel read inside 60 seconds.
✔ Green light

When a real person from your audience reads it and says "that's exactly me" — the message is locked. Now, and only now, move to Step 2.
