# The Time Bank

- **Placement:** Re-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Execution/Re-Pitch · 4 WHEN
- **Job:** objection-break (time)
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "I don't have time for this right now." (Waiting for a free window that never comes — while the days burn either way.)
- **Soundbyte:** "You're spending the day no matter what. Spend it on purpose."
- **Source:** Adapted from the widely-circulated "86,400 seconds / bank of time" parable (public, loosely attributed to Marc Levy). Retold original.
- **Runtime:** ~2–3 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"How many of you want this, you believe it works, but the thing in your head is *I just
don't have the time right now*? Be honest. Type **that's me.**"

## 2 · STORY
"Okay. Imagine a bank. And every single morning, this bank drops **86,400** into your
account. Everybody say **86,400.**

But there are rules. You can't save it. You can't roll it over to tomorrow. Whatever you
don't spend by midnight… it's gone. Burned. And the next morning, fresh again — 86,400.

Now — what would you do? Type it. You'd spend every last bit of it, right? You'd never
let a dollar burn.

Here's the thing. You have that account. It's real. It's not dollars — it's the 86,400
**seconds** in every day. Deposited each morning. Gone each night. No savings, no
rollover, no refunds.

You're not deciding *whether* to spend today's 86,400. That's already spent by
midnight, guaranteed. You're only deciding **what you spent it on.**"

## 3 · TURN
"How many of you keep spending today's seconds on the exact same day you had last year —
telling yourself you'll start when there's more time?

There is never going to be more time. There's just the same 86,400, over and over, spent
on the old life or the new one. 'I don't have time' really means 'I'm choosing to spend
it on something else.'"

## 4 · TARGET
Breaks the "no time" objection by exposing time as a fixed daily spend, not a savings
account you can wait to fill. It converts "later, when I have time" into "I'm choosing
the old life with today's hours" — and makes starting now the only logical move.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"You're spending the day no matter what. Spend it on purpose."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So let's put today's 86,400 toward the life you actually want. Here's where the first
seconds go."

---

### Placement notes
The "I don't have time" objection bit — runs late, near the close. Keep the number
exact (86,400 seconds in a day) and the energy high on the call-and-response.
