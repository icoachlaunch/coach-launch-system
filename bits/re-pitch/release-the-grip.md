# Release the Grip

- **Placement:** Re-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Execution/Re-Pitch · 4 WHEN
- **Job:** objection-break (money / letting go)
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "I don't want to let go of my money." (Clinging to what they have is the very thing keeping them stuck.)
- **Soundbyte:** "Release the grip."
- **Source:** Adapted from the "South Indian monkey trap," made famous by Robert Pirsig in *Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance* (1974) — a public parable about value rigidity. Retold in original words and credited.
- **Runtime:** ~3–4 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"How many of you have ever wondered how you actually catch a monkey? Stay with me —
there's a big point coming. Type **tell me.**"

## 2 · STORY
"There's an old parable — a writer named Pirsig made it famous — about how villagers
trap a monkey.

They don't use a net. They don't chase it. They take a coconut, hollow it out, and
chain it to the ground. Then they cut one hole — just big enough for a monkey's open
hand to slide in. And inside, they put a little rice, or a banana. Everybody say
**banana.**

The monkey smells it. Reaches in. Grabs a fistful. And now here's the trap — the hole
is big enough for an open hand, but not for a **fist.** With the banana in its grip, the
monkey is stuck.

Now watch. There's no net. Nothing holding that monkey but its own hand. It could open
its fingers and walk free any second. But it won't. It pulls, it screams, it fights —
and it will sit there until the trappers come, because the one thing it will not do…
is **let go.**"

## 3 · TURN
"Now, we're all smarter than a monkey. Type **smarter than a monkey.**

But how many of you are holding onto something with a death grip — some money, an old
way of doing things, a comfort — so scared to release it that it's got you trapped in
the exact spot you want out of?

Here's what the monkey can't see. There are infinite bananas in the jungle. It's
squeezing one, and the one it's squeezing is the only thing standing between it and all
the rest."

## 4 · TARGET
Breaks the money-clinging objection at the point of decision. It reframes the price as a
grip they're refusing to open — and the thing they're gripping as the very reason
they're stuck. Makes releasing the money feel like freedom, not loss.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"Release the grip."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So the choice is simple. Keep squeezing the banana you've got — or open your hand and
reach for the whole jungle. Let me show you what's waiting when you let go."

---

### Placement notes
A money/price-objection bit for the close — pairs with any of the three closes at the
price-reveal moment. High-participation; lean into the call-and-response. Keep the
Pirsig credit in so it's clearly a retold parable, not lifted verbatim.
