# The Crab Bucket

- **Placement:** Re-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Execution/Re-Pitch · 4 WHEN
- **Job:** objection-break (external / social)
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "I need to check with the people around me / what will they think?" (The people closest to them talk them out of the leap.)
- **Soundbyte:** "Don't let the crabs in the bucket set your ceiling."
- **Source:** Adapted from the widely-known "crab mentality" / crabs-in-a-bucket folk metaphor (public domain). Retold original.
- **Runtime:** ~2–3 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"How many of you have gotten excited about something — a big move, an investment in
yourself — and the first person you told talked you right out of it? Type **yes.**"

## 2 · STORY
"Let me tell you about crabs. If you catch one crab and put it in a bucket, you have to
put a lid on it — because one crab will climb right out.

But if you put a bunch of crabs in the bucket? No lid needed. Everybody say **no lid.**

Why? Because the second one crab starts climbing toward the top — toward freedom — the
others reach up and pull it back down. Every time. Not one of them gets out. They'd all
rather stay in the bucket together than watch one of them escape."

## 3 · TURN
"How many of you have crabs in your bucket?

The people who mean well. Who love you. But every time you reach for something bigger,
they say *who do you think you are? That's risky. That's not for people like us. Stay
down here where it's safe with the rest of us.*

They're not evil. They're just reporting the view from inside the bucket. But if you let
people at the bottom set your ceiling, you'll never climb out."

## 4 · TARGET
Breaks the external / social-approval objection ("I have to run it by my spouse, my
friends, my circle") at the close. It gives them permission to stop letting other
people's fear dictate their decision — without insulting the people they love.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"Don't let the crabs in the bucket set your ceiling."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So here's the real question — are you deciding this, or are you letting the bucket
decide it for you? Let me show you what climbing out actually looks like."

---

### Placement notes
The go-to bit for the "what will people think / I need to ask someone" objection.
Runs late, near the close. Keep the tone compassionate — the crabs are loved ones, not
enemies; the point is *their view isn't your ceiling,* not *cut everyone off.*
