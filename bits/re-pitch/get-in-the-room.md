# Get in the Room

- **Placement:** Re-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Execution/Re-Pitch · 4 WHEN (also Remove Risk · 3 WHY)
- **Job:** belief-break
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "I can figure this out on my own with enough information / free content." (Info-hoarding as a substitute for guidance and proximity.)
- **Soundbyte:** "You don't need more information. You need to get in the room."
- **Source:** Original. Uses the common, true fact that Everest climbers summit with experienced guides (Sherpas) — no invented numbers.
- **Runtime:** ~3–4 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"Raise a hand — or type **me** — if you've bought the courses, watched the videos, saved
the posts… and you still haven't gotten the result you wanted."

## 2 · STORY
"Yeah. A lot of hands. So let me ask you something.

If we got rich from reading books, librarians would be the wealthiest people on earth.
Have you ever seen a librarian pull up in a Ferrari? They've got access to every book
ever written. So why not?

Because information alone doesn't do it.

Let me make it real. Imagine I hand you a book — *How to Climb Mount Everest.* Great
book. You read the whole thing. Everybody say **book.**

Now, good idea or bad idea for you to fly out and climb Everest tomorrow? Type it.

Right. Bad idea. Okay, so you don't stop there. You buy the boots, the oxygen, the gear.
You've got the info AND the equipment now. Better idea or still a bad idea? Type it.

Still bad. You'd die on that mountain and you know it.

Now — what if you climbed it next to a guide who has summited it many times? Someone who
knows every ridge, every storm, when to move, when to stop, and reaches back and grabs
your hand at the parts that kill people. Are your chances better now? Type **yes.**

Not a little better. A thousand times better. Because they know the things the book
can't tell you — the tiny stuff, the timing, the *don't step there.*"

## 3 · TURN
"How many of you have been trying to climb your mountain from a book?

Information alone doesn't get the result. Information plus action, all by yourself,
still doesn't get it. The people who actually make it are the ones who get in the room
with someone already standing on top."

## 4 · TARGET
Breaks the DIY / "just need more free content" belief that stops people from investing
in guidance. It reframes the offer (coaching, mentorship, the program) as the guide on
the mountain — the missing variable — right before the close.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"You don't need more information. You need to get in the room."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So the only question is which room you get in. Let me show you the one I built for you."

---

### Placement notes
A core close-primer whenever the offer is high-touch (coaching/consulting) — pairs with
the Strategy and Deposit closes. Run it just before the Value Stack / offer reveal.
