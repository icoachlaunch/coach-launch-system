# Down Before Up

- **Placement:** Re-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Remove Risk · 3 WHY (into Execution · 4 WHEN)
- **Job:** belief-break
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "Growth should feel comfortable, and if it's hard or I dip first, something's wrong." (Fear of the dip stops them from committing to real change.)
- **Soundbyte:** "You have to go down before you go up."
- **Source:** Adapted from two public natural-world archetypes — the seed that must break to become a tree, and the eaglet that learns to fly by falling. Retold original.
- **Runtime:** ~3–4 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"Quick one. How many of you have quit something the moment it got uncomfortable — told
yourself *this shouldn't be this hard, maybe it's not for me*? Type **been there.**"

## 2 · STORY
"Let me show you a law nobody talks about. What goes up… must come ______. Type it.
Down. Right — Newton.

But here's the part they leave out. What goes up must go **down first.** Watch. Before
you jump, your body dips. Before a plane climbs, it races *down* the runway. Before a
skyscraper goes up, they dig a hole for the foundation. Down first. Always.

Now look at a seed. This little seed wants to be a big tree. What does it have to do
first? Type it. It has to go **down** — into the dirt, the dark, the cold. And it has to
stop being a seed. It cracks open. It looks like it's dying. That's the exact moment
it's becoming a tree.

Same with an eagle. You know how a mother eagle teaches her eaglet to fly? She carries
it up high on her back… and then she drops it. And that little eaglet falls — feathers
and fluff tumbling through the sky — sure it's the end. And right before it hits, she
swoops under and catches it. Again. And again. Until one day the wind catches its wings,
and it flies.

Eagles learn to fly by **falling first.**"

## 3 · TURN
"How many of you have been standing at the edge of a real change — and pulled back the
second it started to feel like falling?

That's most people. They go all in on themselves, they start to dip, and it's so
uncomfortable they grab the nearest branch and climb back into the same old nest. Safe.
Stuck. Forever.

We're not afraid of falling. We're afraid of hitting the ground. So get yourself a
mother eagle — someone who's going to catch you before you hit."

## 4 · TARGET
Reframes the discomfort and short-term dip of real change as the mechanism of growth,
not a warning sign. It pre-empts "this feels risky/hard" at the close and positions the
offer as the mother eagle — the one who catches them through the dip.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"You have to go down before you go up."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So don't grab the branch this time. Let me show you who's going to catch you while you
learn to fly."

---

### Placement notes
Belief-break for the fear-of-the-dip / fear-of-hard objection — strong just before the
offer. Positions high-touch support (the "mother eagle") as the risk-remover, so it
pairs naturally with the Strategy and Deposit closes.
