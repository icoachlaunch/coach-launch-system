# Giraffes vs Turtles

- **Placement:** Re-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Execution/Re-Pitch · 4 WHEN
- **Job:** objection-break (external / social)
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "The people around me doubt this / I'm worried what others will think."
- **Soundbyte:** "You can't explain a giraffe decision to a turtle."
- **Source:** Repurposed from your example (raw: `_source-examples/_originals/re-pitch/Giraffes and Turtles.docx`). Credits TD Jakes. Your doc already includes an engagement-layered version — that's the one built out below.
- **Runtime:** ~3–4 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"How many of you have ever allowed other people to influence important decisions in your
life? Or worried about what others might think of something you posted? Type **yes.**"

## 2 · STORY
"Giraffes and turtles occupy the same space but don't have the same world view. The
giraffe can stand in the exact same spot as the turtle — but the giraffe eats from the
tops of the trees, and the turtle moves through the grass.

That's what happens in the life of all people: we eat on the level of our vision.

When you're a giraffe and you get criticism from turtles, the turtles are just reporting
the view from the level they're on. (How many of you have had people give you advice who
weren't on your level? Maybe it was well-intended — but it wasn't helping you, it was
holding you back.)

If you understand this, you won't be moved by what they say. It's not that they're always
wrong — they know who you *were.* But their poor understanding of who you are will
incarcerate you to who you were, because they keep dragging you back to your lowest self
instead of your highest.

Here's why the giraffe: a giraffe has a 25-pound heart to pump blood up its 9-foot neck
so its head can stay high. If the giraffe lowers its head to the turtle's level, it
passes out. When you're built to be tall, you endanger your position by lowering your
perspective.

There's nothing wrong with the turtle — it's giving an honest report from where it is.
But you can't let people with a turtle's view control what you see from up at giraffe
level. The turtle can't reach up — so you can't explain a giraffe decision to a turtle."

## 3 · TURN
"So all those people telling you *'if I were you, I wouldn't…'* — that's not you. Stay on
your level. Their view isn't your ceiling."

## 4 · TARGET
Breaks the external / social-approval objection at the close ("I need to run this by
people") without insulting the people they love — reframes doubters as reporting a view
from a lower vantage, not delivering truth about the buyer's potential.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"You can't explain a giraffe decision to a turtle."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So this is a giraffe decision. Let me show you what it looks like from up here."
