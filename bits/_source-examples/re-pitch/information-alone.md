# Information Alone

- **Placement:** Re-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Execution/Re-Pitch · 4 WHEN
- **Job:** belief-break
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "If I just learn enough / get more information, I'll get the result."
- **Soundbyte:** "Get in the room with people already doing the thing."
- **Source:** Repurposed from your example (raw: `_source-examples/_originals/re-pitch/Information Alone BITS.docx`). Credits Austin Ford.
- **Runtime:** ~3–4 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"Raise your hand and let me know you're still with me! Quick one — how many of you have
learned a TON of content and still didn't get the result? Type **me.**"

## 2 · STORY
"We learn a lot of content. And the belief underneath it is: *if I have enough education,
then I can get the result. If I learn the information, then I get the outcome.*

I've said it once and I'll say it again — if we got rich by reading books, we'd never
leave the library. Have any of you ever seen a librarian riding around in a Ferrari? Why
not? They have access to ALL the information — books, Google, YouTube, audiobooks.

Here's a book: *How to Climb Mount Everest.* 29,000 feet. How many of you think — 'I read
this, I've got a good chance of going and climbing that mountain'? Good idea or bad idea?

Okay, so it's information PLUS action. I read the book, I bought the equipment, I got my
plane ticket. Still a good idea or a bad idea?

But what if I reached out to someone who's summited Everest many times? They make me part
of the team. They tell me what to do and when to do it. Now I know they can't guarantee
my safety — but do you think my chances just went up a thousand times? Yes or no? Why?

Because there are little details you miss, little nuances you need help with."

## 3 · TURN
"So information alone doesn't solve it. Information plus action, on your own, doesn't
solve it either. The people who get the REAL results are the ones who get in the room
with people already doing the thing. And that changes everything. If you believe that,
say **YES** — 'I get in the room.'"

## 4 · TARGET
Breaks the DIY / "more content will fix it" belief right before the offer — reframing
the high-touch program as the guide on the mountain, the missing variable between effort
and results.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"Get in the room with people already doing the thing."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"Now a lot of you are asking — what's next? Let me show you the room I built for you."
