# The Two Biggest Expenses

- **Placement:** Re-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Execution/Re-Pitch · 4 WHEN
- **Job:** close-prime (cost of inaction)
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "How much is this going to cost me?" (Reframes price against the far bigger cost of not knowing.)
- **Soundbyte:** "Your biggest expense is what it costs you to NOT know."
- **Source:** Repurposed from your example (raw: `_source-examples/_originals/re-pitch/Two Biggest Expenses BITS.docx`). Credits Myron Golden.
- **Runtime:** ~4–5 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"You're all in business, correct? Give me a **yes** in the chat. One of the most
dangerous things in business isn't just not making enough income — it's expenses.
Everybody say **expenses!**"

## 2 · STORY
"There are two biggest expenses to watch out for. I'll start with the SECOND one.

What do you think the second biggest expense in your business is? (Call and response.)
**Taxes.** Most people never put it on the list — because they nickel-and-dime us to
death. You think you're at a 15, 20, 30% rate. But look: income tax, social security tax,
sales tax, capital gains, property tax, death tax. You get taxed over and over on the
same money. When it's all said and done, you hand about 50 cents of every dollar to Uncle
Sam. $5 of every $10. $50 of every $100. $500,000 of every million.

But if TAXES are only the second biggest expense… what could possibly be the FIRST?
What's even bigger than 50%?

Let me ask you something first. Anyone here know how to bake a chocolate cake? Could you
show me how? (Bring them in.) What that tells us is: one intelligent person can teach
another intelligent person to repeat what they did. Yes?

So here's the number nobody counts. Your current biggest expense is what it costs you to
NOT know how to make [the result — e.g. a million dollars a year]. That's what you paid
life last year for not knowing, and for not having the support, to get there."

## 3 · TURN
"So the only question is: are you willing to pay that again next year — or are you ready
to do what's necessary to turn it around?

The question isn't 'do I have the money,' 'do I have the time,' 'will my spouse let me.'
The question is: are you willing to pay LIFE that same bill again next year?"

## 4 · TARGET
Reframes price as a comparison — a small known cost versus the huge, recurring, invisible
cost of staying the same. Makes the cost of inaction the biggest number in the room right
before the price reveal.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"Your biggest expense is what it costs you to NOT know."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"Stop paying the biggest expense of all. It's your turn to win — let me show you how we
turn it around."

Close-primer for the price reveal — pairs with every close. Have the attendee name their
