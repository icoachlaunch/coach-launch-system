# Monkey Paw (Release the Banana)

- **Placement:** Re-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Execution/Re-Pitch · 4 WHEN
- **Job:** objection-break (money / letting go)
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "I don't want to let go of the money I'm holding."
- **Soundbyte:** "Release the bananas."
- **Source:** Repurposed from your examples (raw: `_source-examples/_originals/re-pitch/Monkey Paw - Joseph Aaron - Original Video Transcription.docx` and `Monkey Paw - Prince EA.docx`). Two proven retellings of the same bit; Joseph Aaron's is the fuller build below.
- **Runtime:** ~4–5 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"How many of you have ever wondered how to capture a monkey? Stay with me — I promise
there's a big point at the end that's going to help you. Type **tell me.**"

## 2 · STORY
"I love monkeys. Cute, cuddly, running around. But I read something about how they trap
one.

They build a heavy wooden box. On one side they cut a circular hole — a specific diameter,
on purpose. Inside, they place something the monkey wants. What would a monkey want? Put
it in the chat. Bananas — everybody says bananas.

So they put a banana in the box, close it, and set it in the jungle. The monkey smells
it, runs around, looks in, and reaches his hand through the hole to grab the banana. And
as he starts to pull it out — he can't. They cut the hole just so a hand fits in, but a
FIST holding a banana can't come out. The monkey is trapped. Everybody say **trapped.**

And here's the crazy thing — the monkey will not release the banana. He'll squeeze, pull
harder, scream his head off. The one thing he won't consider doing is letting go. If I
saw him I'd say, 'Monkey — release the banana!' Say it with me: release the banana."

## 3 · TURN
"Now, we're all smarter than a monkey. But how many of you have found yourself holding
onto what you already have — afraid that if you release it, you won't get the reward?

Some of you are gripping old ideas. Some, old paradigms. Some are gripping money in your
hands — it would just take a small investment to go to the next level, but you don't want
to release what you have. And it's the very gripping of what you already have that's
trapping you from everything you could have.

There are an infinite number of bananas in the jungle. All that monkey has to do to
access the ones he doesn't have… is release the one he's holding."

## 4 · TARGET
Breaks the money-clinging objection at the price reveal — reframes the price as a grip
they're refusing to open, and the thing they're gripping as the very reason they're
stuck. Makes releasing the money feel like freedom, not loss.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"Release the bananas."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So there are some bananas you need to release to get to the next level. Give yourselves
a big round of applause — you're smarter than a monkey. Now let me show you what's waiting
when you let go."
