# THE SPEAKER BIT LIBRARY

A growing library of **speaker bits** — timeless, repeatable stories you slot into a
Sniper Presentation™ to raise emotion and drive results.

A bit isn't filler. It's a story that does ONE job at ONE moment: build engagement,
create hype, break a limiting belief, break an external objection, or prime the close.
Story is the driver. Emotion makes the sale. These are the units that carry the emotion.

---

## HOW THE LIBRARY IS ORGANIZED

Two folders, by **placement** in the presentation:

- **`pre-pitch/`** — the front half of the event (Phases 1–2, WHO / WHAT).
  Jobs: engagement, hype, empowering belief. These warm the room and open minds
  BEFORE the offer.

- **`re-pitch/`** — the back half, around the close (Phases 3–4, WHY / WHEN).
  Jobs: break a limiting belief, break an external objection, prime the buying decision.
  These are the stories that fill your card's **Belief / Bust It** (WHY) and
  **Objection / Handle It** (WHEN) rows.

> There is no `pitch/` folder. The pitch itself IS the Sniper Close™ sequence —
> it's engineered for the specific offer, not a swappable story.

Each bit is one `.md` file. The filename is a short slug (e.g. `release-the-grip.md`).

## WHERE BITS SIT IN THE SCORE™ SPINE

The Sniper Presentation™ runs on the five SCORE™ acts. Every act has story slots:

| SCORE™ act | Phase (card) | Bit jobs that live here |
|---|---|---|
| Serendipity + Connection | 1 · WHO | engagement · hype · empowering belief |
| Opportunity | 2 · WHAT | engagement · desire / transformation |
| Remove Risk | 3 · WHY | belief-break · proof |
| Execution / Re-Pitch | 4 · WHEN | objection-break · close-prime |

Length flexes on bits: a **Masterclass** (single day) runs a lean set; a **Challenge**
(3-day) expands by adding more bits into the same acts — story stretches the length
without changing the structure.

## THE 6-PART BIT ANATOMY

Every bit — reused or newly written — follows the same skeleton (pulled from proven
bits). Build new ones from `_bit-template.md`.

1. **HOOK** — an engagement setup. A raised-hand question, a "how many of you…", a
   "say it in the chat." Pattern-interrupt + call-and-response. Gets them leaning in.
2. **STORY** — the metaphor or narrative, delivered WITH participation baked in
   ("everybody say ___", fill-in-the-blank, a demonstration). Vivid and simple.
3. **TURN** — the bridge from the story to THEIR life. "How many of you have found
   yourself…" This is where the metaphor becomes about them.
4. **TARGET** — the exact belief or objection the bit breaks. Named up top so you
   always know why the bit is in the room.
5. **SOUNDBYTE** — the one repeatable line they'll remember and say back
   ("release the grip", "you have to fail to win").
6. **TRANSITION** — hands off to the next beat or into the close.

## THE GOLDEN RULE — WHOSE STORY IS IT?

Keep this line clean, always:

- **Teaching metaphors, parables, and archetypes** (the monkey trap, the eagle, the
  crab bucket) are shared. Retell them in your own words and **credit** where they
  came from ("there's an old parable…", "Pirsig wrote about…"). Every bit in this
  library is written original for that reason — safe for you to use and distribute.
- **A personal story** — told as something that happened to YOU — must be true and
  yours. Never borrow someone's life and tell it as your own. If you don't have a
  personal story for a slot, use a metaphor bit or build your own from the template.

Never invent statistics. Where a bit cites a real fact or quote, it's verified and
sourced in the bit file. If you can't verify a number, cut it — the story carries the
weight, not the stat.

## HOW TO USE A BIT

1. Pick the slot you're filling (act + job + the belief/objection in the room).
2. Grab the bit whose **Target** matches — or write a new one from the template.
3. Re-point the **TURN** and **SOUNDBYTE** at your avatar and your offer.
4. Rehearse it until it's repeatable. A bit is an asset — the more you run it, the
   better it lands.

Add your own bits anytime. Drop new `.md` files into `pre-pitch/` or `re-pitch/`
using the template. The library is meant to grow.
