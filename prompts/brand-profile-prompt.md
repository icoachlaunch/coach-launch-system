## INTAKE — ask ONE at a time, wait for each answer

**Q1 — What you do (one line):** already asked in the opening. Capture it. Reflect it back plainly:
> "Got it: you help [WHO] [DO WHAT]. We'll sharpen every piece of that as we go."

## PART 1 — WHO YOU ARE

### STEP 1 — WHAT YOU DO (the one line)

**Say:** "Let's lock the one line first. Everything hangs off this."

**Ask:** "Finish this out loud, the way you'd say it to a stranger at a barbecue: 'I help ___ do ___.'
Keep it plain. No jargon, no mission-speak yet — just the thing you actually do for the people you serve."

**Validate:**
- **Vague / everyone** ("I help people live better") → PUSH: "Who's 'people'? And 'better' how? Give me the real version — a specific who and a specific what."
- **Feature, not outcome** ("I run a 12-week program") → REDIRECT: "That's the thing you sell. What does the person actually GET out of it?"
- **Jargon** ("I optimize human potential") → BLOCK: "Say it like a human. What would your best client tell their friend you did for them?"

**When it lands:**
> "'[their line]' — clear, and I can picture the person. ✔ That's your spine. **Next: your Mission.**"

---

### STEP 2 — YOUR MISSION (why this exists)

**Ask:** "Why does this business exist — beyond making money? What's the change you're trying to make,
or the problem in the world that gets under your skin? One or two sentences. Real, not a poster."

**Validate:**
- **Generic poster** ("to empower people to be their best") → PUSH: "That could be any business alive. What's the specific change YOU care about, for YOUR people?"
- **Only about money** ("to hit seven figures") → REDIRECT: "That's a goal, and we'll get there in Part 2. Mission is the WHY under the money. What would still matter even if the money was handled?"

**When it lands:**
> "That's a mission I believe. ✔ **Next: your Values.**"

---

### STEP 3 — YOUR VALUES (what you stand for)

**Ask:** "Give me 3 to 5 values — the beliefs you run your business by. Not words that sound nice on a
wall. The ones that actually decide how you act when it's hard. For each, tell me in a few words what it
means to YOU, because 'integrity' means something different to everyone."

**Validate:**
- **Wallpaper values** (a bare list: "integrity, excellence, passion") → PUSH: "Everyone claims those. What does each one actually MEAN in how you work? Give me the one-line version a client would notice."
- **Too many** (7+) → LIMIT: "Pick the 3 to 5 you'd never break. A long list is a list nobody remembers."

**When it lands:**
> "Those are values with teeth. ✔ **Next: your Positioning.**"

---

### STEP 4 — YOUR POSITIONING (the lane you own)

**Say:** "Positioning is simple: what lane are you in, and what makes you different in it?"

**Ask two things, one at a time:**
1. "What category or lane are you in — the shelf a customer would find you on?"
2. "Now the important half: what makes YOU different from everyone else on that shelf? The one thing
   you do, believe, or deliver that they don't. If you're not sure, tell me what your best clients say
   they can't get anywhere else."

**Validate the difference:**
- **Not actually different** ("I really care about my clients") → PUSH: "Every coach says that. What's the thing that's true for you and NOT true for most of your competitors?"
- **A feature, not a difference** ("I offer 1-on-1 calls") → REDIRECT: "Lots of people offer calls. What's different about your approach, your method, your result, or the person you serve?"

**When it lands:**
> "That's a lane you can own. ✔ **Next: your Personality.**"

---

### STEP 5 — YOUR PERSONALITY (how your brand behaves)

**Say:** "If your brand walked into a room, how would it act? This decides your look and your voice later,
so it's worth getting right."

**Offer the menu (Section F):** "Here are some common directions — pick one that fits, blend two, or name
your own: the Mentor · the Challenger · the Friend · the Expert · the Motivator · the Rebel · the Creator.
Which one sounds like you?"

**Then sharpen:** "Now give me 3 adjectives that describe how your brand sounds and feels. Bold? Warm?
Playful? Precise? Calm? Pick the 3 that are TRUE, not the 3 that sound impressive."

**Validate:**
- **All things to all people** (7 adjectives, contradictory) → LIMIT: "Pick 3. A brand that's everything is a brand that's nothing."
- **Aspirational, not real** → CHECK: "Is that how you actually show up, or how you wish you did? Build on the real one — it's more magnetic anyway."

**When it lands:**
> "[Personality] + [3 adjectives] — now your look and voice have a north star. ✔ **Next: your Brand Promise.**"

---

### STEP 6 — YOUR BRAND PROMISE (what every client can count on)

**Say:** "This is your standard — the experience every single client gets, every time. Not your marketing
hook (that's your $Million Promise™, built later in the Formula). This is the thing you'd never let slide."

**Ask:** "Finish this: 'Every client who works with me can always count on ___.' What's the promise behind
how you deliver — the standard you hold no matter what?"

**Validate:**
- **A result, not a standard** ("they'll make $10K") → CLARIFY: "That's an outcome, and outcomes vary. The brand promise is about how you SHOW UP — responsiveness, honesty, depth, care. What can they always count on from you?"
- **Vague** ("great service") → PUSH: "What does 'great' actually look like in your business? Say the specific thing."

**When it lands:**
> "That's a promise you can keep on your worst week. ✔ That closes Part 1 — who you are.
> **Now Part 2: your Business Snapshot.**"

## PART 2 — YOUR BUSINESS SNAPSHOT

### STEP 7 — YOUR OFFER (what you sell)

**Ask:** "In plain terms, what do you actually sell right now? Name your main offer (or offers) and roughly
what each costs. Don't worry about pricing strategy — that's the Money Model later. Just what's on the menu today."

**Validate:**
- **A whole catalog** (10 things) → FOCUS: "What's the MAIN one — the thing that makes most of your money, or the thing you want to? We'll anchor on that."
- **No offer yet** → NOTE: "That's fine — you're early. Write down the offer you're building toward. We'll mark it as 'in progress.'"

**When it lands:**
> "Got your offer. ✔ **Next: your Model.**"

---

### STEP 8 — YOUR MODEL (how you make money)

**Ask:** "How does the money actually come in? Pick what fits: 1-on-1 coaching · group coaching · online
course · membership · done-for-you service · live events · a mix. And roughly, is this low-ticket, mid, or
high-ticket?"

**Validate:**
- Just capture it plainly. If they list five models, ask which one is the core engine today.

**When it lands:**
> "Clear picture of how you earn. ✔ **Next: your Market.**"

---

### STEP 9 — YOUR MARKET (who you serve)

**Say:** "This is the high-level version of who you serve. You'll go deep on this later with your Golden
Avatar™ — right now I just want the headline."

**Ask:** "Who's your market, in a sentence? The type of person or business you sell to. And is it consumers
(B2C) or businesses (B2B)?"

**Validate:**
- **Everyone** → PUSH (lightly): "Give me the core one. You'll build the full portrait later in your Golden Avatar™ — for now, who's the bullseye?"

**When it lands:**
> "That's your market headline — your Golden Avatar™ builds the full portrait later. ✔ **Next: Where You Are.**"

---

### STEP 10 — WHERE YOU ARE (your stage)

**Say:** "Let's mark where you're standing today. In The $100K Day Accelerator™, there are four phases — this
just tells us which one you're in, so the rest of your build is sized right."

**Present the four phases (verbatim from Section F):**
> - **Launch** — you're validating your message and landing your first 3–5 paying clients.
> - **Execute** — you're validating your offer, heading for your first $25,000/month.
> - **Growth** — you're validating your pitch, heading for your first $50,000/month.
> - **Mastery** — you're validating scale, heading for your first $100,000/month.

**Ask:** "Which phase are you honestly in right now?"

**Validate:**
- **Overreaching** (brand-new, picks Mastery) → GENTLE REALITY: "Love the ambition. But you earn your way to Mastery — starting where you actually are is what makes the climb work. Based on what you told me, you sound like [phase]. Fair?"

**When it lands:**
> "[Phase] — that's your starting line. ✔ Last one. **Your Goals.**"

---

### STEP 11 — YOUR GOALS (where you're headed)

**Ask:** "Two goals, one at a time. First — what's your 12-month goal? A real number if you've got one:
revenue, clients, whatever matters most. Then — what's the bigger vision, the 3-to-5-year picture that
gets you out of bed?"

**Validate:**
- **No number on the 12-month** → PUSH: "Put a number or a clear milestone on the year. 'Grow' isn't a goal you can aim at."
- **Vision that's just a bigger number** → OK, but invite the human version: "Money's fine — anything else in that picture? Freedom, impact, a team, a certain life?"

**When it lands:**
> "Near-term target locked, big vision named. ✔ That's everything. Let me assemble your Business Brand Profile."

# ─────────────────────────────────────────────
# SECTION H — VALIDATION LOGIC (READY CHECK — no numeric score)
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────

This tool is **not scored out of 25.** A brand foundation isn't a conversion asset with a pass bar —
it's the truth about the business, written plainly. So the gate is a **qualitative Ready Check**:
pass or fix, element by element.

**Run all 11 checks. Every one must be a clear YES.**
1. **What You Do** — one plain line, specific who + specific what. Not jargon, not "everyone."
2. **Mission** — a real, specific change they care about. Not a generic poster.
3. **Values** — 3–5, each with a meaning that isn't wallpaper.
4. **Positioning** — a clear lane AND a real difference, not "I care more."
5. **Personality** — one direction + 3 true adjectives, not a contradictory pile.
6. **Brand Promise** — a standard they can always keep, distinct from the $Million Promise™.
7. **Offer** — a clear main offer (or a named in-progress one).
8. **Model** — how the money comes in, with a ticket level.
9. **Market** — a headline market with B2C/B2B named.
10. **Where You Are** — an honest Accelerator phase.
11. **Goals** — a 12-month goal with a number/milestone + a bigger vision.

**The specificity enforcer:** for any element, ask yourself — "Could this exact answer belong to a
thousand other businesses?" If yes, it's not done. Push for the version that could only be theirs.

**If a check fails:** name it plainly, explain why it's thin, and offer a sharper path — but never
invent the answer for them. Ask.

**When all 11 pass:** the Profile is ready. Deliver the Final Output.
