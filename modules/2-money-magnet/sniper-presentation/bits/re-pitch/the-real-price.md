# The Real Price

- **Placement:** Re-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Execution/Re-Pitch · 4 WHEN
- **Job:** close-prime (cost of inaction)
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "This costs too much." (They're weighing the price of the offer while ignoring the far bigger price they're already paying to stay the same.)
- **Soundbyte:** "You're already paying. The only question is for what."
- **Source:** Original
- **Runtime:** ~3 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"Let me ask the question that's in the back of some of your minds right now. *What's this
going to cost me?* Fair question. Type **fair.** So let's actually do the math — the real
math."

## 2 · STORY
"Most people, when they look at an offer, they see one number. The price. And they go,
*ooh, that's a lot.*

But there's a number nobody puts on the table. It's invisible. It doesn't send you a
bill. But you pay it every single year, on time, without fail.

It's the cost of staying exactly where you are.

Think about it. What did last year cost you? Not in dollars you spent — in the result
you didn't get. The clients you didn't sign. The income you left sitting there. The
year of your life you traded doing it the hard way, alone, guessing.

Put a number on that. Go on — what's it actually worth? That number is enormous. And
here's the brutal part: you already paid it. Last year. And you'll pay it again this
year. And next year. Quietly. Automatically. For as long as nothing changes."

## 3 · TURN
"So the question was never *can I afford this?* You've been affording the expensive
option this whole time — the do-nothing option.

The real question is: are you willing to pay that invisible bill one more year? Or are
you ready to trade it for a number that's a fraction of the size and actually gets you
out?"

## 4 · TARGET
Reframes price as a comparison — not "spend vs. save," but "small known cost vs. huge
recurring hidden cost." It makes the cost of inaction the biggest number in the room, so
the offer's price feels like the cheap option. A close-primer for the price reveal.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"You're already paying. The only question is for what."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So let's stop paying for standing still. Here's exactly what it looks like to switch
which bill you're paying — starting today."

---

### Placement notes
A close-primer for the price-reveal moment — pairs with every close. Run it right before
you name the price. Do not invent specific dollar figures for the "invisible bill" — make
the attendee name their own; that's what makes it land and keeps it honest.
