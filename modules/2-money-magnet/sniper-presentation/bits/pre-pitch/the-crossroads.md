# The Crossroads

- **Placement:** Pre-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Opportunity · 2 WHAT
- **Job:** empowering-belief
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "I'll decide later / I need more time to think." (Chronic indecision, dressed up as being careful, is the real thing keeping them stuck.)
- **Soundbyte:** "Not deciding is a decision — it just picks the worst option for you."
- **Source:** Original
- **Runtime:** ~2–3 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"Be honest with me. How many of you have something in your life you've been *thinking
about* doing for way too long? Months. Maybe years. Type **guilty**."

## 2 · STORY
"Let me paint you a picture. Imagine you're standing at a crossroads. One road is the
life you've got now. The other is the life you actually want.

And you stand there. And you think about it. You research it. You ask your cousin, your
neighbor, three strangers on the internet. You wait for the perfect moment, the perfect
sign, the perfect amount of money.

Now here's the part nobody tells you. While you're standing there thinking — the sun is
going down. Time is passing. You're not staying still. You're getting older, standing at
the same crossroads, watching the light fade.

Standing still feels safe. It feels careful. But it's not. You didn't avoid a choice.
You chose the road you're already on — by default."

## 3 · TURN
"How many of you have talked yourself out of a decision so many times you forgot you
even wanted the other road?

That's the trap. It doesn't feel like quitting. It feels responsible. But indecision is
just quitting in slow motion."

## 4 · TARGET
Exposes "I'll think about it" as a decision that defaults to the status quo. It plants
the belief that action beats endless deliberation — so that when the offer and the
close arrive, "let me think about it" has already been named as the enemy.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"Not deciding is a decision — it just picks the worst option for you."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So today, you're a decider. Type **decider**. Because what I'm about to show you only
works for the people who actually choose a road."

---

### Placement notes
Strong in the Opportunity act to build a decisive frame early. It also seeds the close:
the "I'll think about it" objection is far weaker later if you've run this up front.
