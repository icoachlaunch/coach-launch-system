# Train the Room

- **Placement:** Pre-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Serendipity+Connection · 1 WHO
- **Job:** engagement
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "I'll just sit back, watch, and take notes." (Passive attendees don't buy — a quiet room is a dead room.)
- **Soundbyte:** "You don't watch this. You do this."
- **Source:** Original
- **Runtime:** ~2 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"Quick question before we start. How many of you actually want to walk away from today
different — not just with more notes, but changed? Type **I'm in** in the chat right now."

## 2 · STORY
"Here's the deal. I'm not a lecturer. Have you ever sat through a lecture and forgotten
it by lunch? Drop a **yes** if that's you.

That's not your fault. That's how watching works. You watch, you nod, you leave, and it's
gone by dinner.

So we're not going to do that today. We're going to do this together. I'm going to ask
you questions, and when I do, I want you typing in the chat. Sometimes I'll start a
sentence and let you finish it. So if I say the more you put in, the more you get
______ — you type it. Go ahead, type it now: **out.**

See that? You're already doing it."

## 3 · TURN
"Here's why this matters. The people who show up quiet, arms crossed, just watching —
they leave the same way they came. Nothing changes.

The people who lean in, who answer, who get their hands dirty — those are the people
things actually shift for. Every single time."

## 4 · TARGET
Breaks passive-attendee mode at the very top. A room that's answering, typing, and
saying words out loud is a room that's awake, bonded to you, and primed to say yes
later. This bit sets the rule for the whole event and makes every later engagement
prompt land.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"You don't watch this. You do this."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So that's the deal for today — you show up as a doer, I'll show up with everything I've
got. Fair? Type **fair**. Good. Let's get into it."

---

### Placement notes
Run this near the very start, right after the hook and welcome — before any teaching.
It's the "train the chat" moment. Works for every phase and every close. Keep it fast
and high-energy; it sets the tone. Note: it deliberately avoids the disputed
"retain 10%/70%" learning-pyramid stats — the point stands on its own without them.
