# Take the Shot

- **Placement:** Pre-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Serendipity+Connection · 1 WHO
- **Job:** empowering-belief
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "I'm scared to fail, so I don't start." (Fear of failure keeps them frozen before they ever try.)
- **Soundbyte:** "You miss every shot you don't take."
- **Source:** Original. Cites the verified Michael Jordan line from the 1990 Nike "Failure" ad (written by Jamie Barrett); Edison's lightbulb attempts are common public lore — told loosely, no hard count.
- **Runtime:** ~3 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"Let me ask you something. How many of you have held back from something — a launch, an
offer, a post — because a little voice said *what if it flops?* Be honest. Type **me**."

## 2 · STORY
"Yeah. Me too. So let me tell you about the best there ever was.

Michael Jordan did a Nike ad back in 1990. And he doesn't brag in it. He counts his
failures. He says: *I've missed over 9,000 shots. I've lost almost 300 games. 26 times
I was trusted to take the game-winning shot — and missed.* And then he says the line:
*I've failed over and over and over again in my life. And that is why I succeed.*

Read that again. The greatest player alive says he's great **because** he failed more
than anyone.

Thomas Edison, same thing. Story goes he ran through thousands of tries before the
lightbulb worked. Somebody asked how it felt to fail that many times. He said he didn't
fail — he found thousands of ways that don't work. Every miss was a step."

## 3 · TURN
"Here's the thing. That voice telling you *don't try, you might look stupid* — everybody
in this room has it. The winners just stopped obeying it.

You're not behind because you failed. You're behind because you haven't taken enough
shots yet. Missing isn't the enemy. Sitting still is."

## 4 · TARGET
Dissolves fear-of-failure paralysis early, so that when the offer comes later, "what if
it doesn't work for me?" has already been reframed as a normal, necessary part of
winning. Turns hesitation into permission to act.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"You miss every shot you don't take."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So today, you're taking the shot. Type **taking the shot** if you're done sitting on
the bench. Good. Let me show you where to aim."

---

### Placement notes
Best early, while building belief and rapport (WHO/Connection). Also works as a
courage bit right before an action step. The Jordan quote is verified — keep it
accurate. Don't add invented numbers to the Edison line; "thousands" is enough.
