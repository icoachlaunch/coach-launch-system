# The Law of Advancement

- **Placement:** Re-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Remove Risk · 3 WHY (into Execution · 4 WHEN)
- **Job:** belief-break
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "To reach the next level it shouldn't require dropping down / going through discomfort first."
- **Soundbyte:** "Eagles learn to fly by falling first."
- **Source:** Repurposed from your example (raw: `_source-examples/_originals/re-pitch/Law of Advancement.docx`). Credits Myron Golden. Uses the seed→tree and mama-eagle archetypes.
- **Runtime:** ~4–5 min
- **Structure used:** 3 parts (the law → the seed → the eagle)

---

## 1 · HOOK
"You all know there are laws in the universe, right? Momentum. Thermodynamics. Relativity.
But there's one that seldom gets talked about — the **Law of Advancement.** Type
**teach me.**"

## 2 · STORY
"**Part 1 — the law.** Fill in the blank: what goes up must come ______. Down — Isaac
Newton. But here's what they don't tell you: what goes up must go DOWN first. Before you
jump, you dip. Before a plane takes off, it races down the runway. Before a building goes
up, they dig the foundation. Everything that goes up must go down first.

**Part 2 — the seed.** This is a seed. And this little seed wants to be a big ol' tree.
What does the seed have to do to become a tree? (Let them answer — 'go down,' 'grow
roots.') In order for a seed to become a tree, it has to cease being a seed. It has to go
down — through the dirt, the grime, the darkness, the discomfort. **You have to stop
being everything you have been in order to become everything you can be.**

**Part 3 — the eagle.** A mama eagle teaches her eaglet to fly by carrying it up high on
her back — 'higher, mama, higher' — and then dropping it. Down the little eaglet goes,
feathers and fluff flying, sure it's the end. And right before it hits the ground —
WHOOSH — mama swoops in and catches it. Again, and again. Until one day a gust of wind
catches its wings… and it flies. Eagles learn to fly by falling first."

## 3 · TURN
"So why don't people reach their full potential? Because people live in a circle of
sameness. There's a point where you take the risk, go all in on yourself — and you begin
to fall. And falling gets so uncomfortable that you grab the lowest branch and climb back
into the circle of sameness, where you stay forever.

We're not afraid of falling. We're afraid of hitting the ground. If you think your income
won't dip before it climbs, you're kidding yourself — you'd be violating the Law of
Advancement."

## 4 · TARGET
Reframes the discomfort and short-term dip of real change as the mechanism of growth, not
a warning sign — and positions high-touch support as the mama eagle who catches them
through the dip. Defuses "this feels risky/hard" right before the offer.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"Eagles learn to fly by falling first."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So don't grab the branch this time. Get yourself a mama eagle who'll catch you before
you hit the ground. Let me show you who that is."
