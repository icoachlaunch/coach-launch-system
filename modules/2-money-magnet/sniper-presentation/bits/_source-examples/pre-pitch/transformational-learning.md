# Transformational Learning

- **Placement:** Pre-Pitch
- **SCORE™ act / phase:** Serendipity+Connection · 1 WHO
- **Job:** engagement
- **Target (the belief/objection it breaks):** "I'll passively watch and take notes." (Passive rooms don't convert.)
- **Soundbyte:** "Information you hear, you forget. Information you say, you keep."
- **Source:** Repurposed from your example (raw: `_source-examples/_originals/pre-pitch/Transformational Learning BITS.docx`). A proven "train the chat" opener.
- **Runtime:** ~2–3 min

---

## 1 · HOOK
"How many of you want to get a TON out of this event and remember as much as possible?
Say **I DO!** in the chat."

## 2 · STORY
"Look, the first thing you need to know is that I'm not a lecturer. Anybody ever been to
a lecture?

I'm going to use a process called Transformational Learning. My mission is not just to
help you get information — it's to help you experience transformation. How many of you
are ready to experience transformation and become a new person who operates at a new
level?

So I'm going to ask a lot of questions. And when I ask, I want you to actually respond in
the chat. Sometimes, like a good preacher, I'll ask you to repeat it — 'somebody say
amen.' Sometimes I'll start a sentence and ask you to fill in the ______.

And there's a reason for that. You retain very little of what you only hear. A bit more
when you write it down. But when you *say* it, when you're active with the information —
something happens. Your retention goes way up. When you speak it, you keep it."

## 3 · TURN
"So the question is: how many of you want to learn more at this event AND keep it longer?
Say **I DO** in the chat. Then you need to get involved. Give yourself a big hand!"

## 4 · TARGET
Sets the engagement contract at the top of the event. A room that's typing, repeating,
and filling in blanks is awake and bonded — which makes every later belief-break and the
close land harder.

## 5 · SOUNDBYTE
"Information you hear, you forget. Information you say, you keep."

## 6 · TRANSITION
"So that's the deal today — you get involved, and you'll walk out changed, not just
informed. Let's go."

---

### Placement notes
Run it right at the top, before teaching. Note: the original leans on specific retention
percentages (10–15% hearing → 70–80% doing). Those numbers come from the "learning
pyramid," which is widely cited but scientifically disputed — the bit works fine on the
principle alone, so keep or cut the exact figures as you prefer.
