What Is the Event Magnet™?

Your Event Magnet™ is a free, visual tool — one hot step of your Magic Formula™ turned into something a stranger can use in a single sitting.

It's not a guide. It's not a report. It's not an educational PDF.

It's a tool — a cheat sheet, a template, a checklist, a script, a scorecard, or a one-page map.

Here's the difference that matters: a guide teaches a concept, and a tool delivers a result.

Your Event Magnet™ is a tool that solves one small but real problem — fast. In minutes, not weeks.

Think of it as the fuse.

The whole payload — your Magic Formula™, your Red Diamond Offer™, the transformation — is inert without something to light it.

The Event Magnet™ is that fuse. It's the first moment of value a cold stranger gets from you, the first proof you can actually help. It's what makes them stop, lean in, and raise their hand to come to your event.

Event Magnet™ = one hot step + a visual format + an immediate win

And it's incomplete on purpose.

It solves ONE problem and leaves them wanting the rest of your system. That's not a flaw — that's the whole design.

Matthew White built Coach Launch on this kind of sequencing — $24 Million in high-ticket sales generated and $5.2 Million in recurring client results delivered, all downstream of free tools that earned the first bit of trust before anyone was ever asked to buy.

The Secret Sauce: the Visual

Here's the insight that changes how you market everything.

The visual creates an open loop. When someone sees an actual THING on their screen, their brain can't ignore it — it has to ask: "What is that? Do I need that? Am I already doing this?"

Words describe. Visuals demand attention.

A one-page map, a checklist, a scorecard — you can hold it up, point at it, show it in an ad or a video. That's what stops the scroll. A block of text never does.

So the rule is simple: if you can't show it on screen, it isn't an Event Magnet™ yet.

What the Event Magnet™ is NOT

It's worth being just as clear about what this isn't, because most people default to one of these and wonder why nobody opts in.

It's not a guide or a report — those teach; they don't hand someone a result they can hold.

It's not your whole system dressed up as "free" — give away everything and there's no reason to buy.

It's not a "free call" or "free consultation" — that asks for their time before you've earned it, and it attracts tire-kickers.

And it's not the offer itself — that's your Red Diamond Offer™. The Event Magnet™ is the free thing that fills the room where you make that offer.

How You'll Build It

You don't invent an Event Magnet™ from a blank page. You build it from a step you've already got.

The Event Magnet™ Builder walks you through it — but understanding the path first makes the build faster and the result stronger.

There are seven moves, in order: confirm your foundation (your Magic Formula™ and promise), confirm your hot step (the seed), pick the format (a tangible tool), build the title (in promise format), build the spec (what's on it and the win it delivers), run the 6-Point Check (pass/fail), and build the CTA (to your event).

Then you walk away with a complete spec — detailed enough that you or a designer could build the finished tool from it.

The rest of this guide teaches each part in full: the two versions, how to find your hot step, the formats, the title, the check, the CTA, and the mistakes that quietly kill conversion.

The Two Versions

There are two ways to build an Event Magnet™. One is the default. One is the exception.

VersionWhat it isWhen to use it
One-Step (default)ONE hot step turned into a cheat sheet, template, checklist, script, calculator, or mapAlmost always. Faster to build, higher conversion, one clean win.
Full System Reveal (advanced)Your whole Magic Formula™ — all 3 stages, 9 steps — as one visual mapOnly when no single step delivers a standalone win (identity or journey work where the steps are sequential).

Default to one step.

The Full System Reveal is tempting because it feels generous — but "here's my whole system" is usually too much, too soon. Reach for it only when you can honestly say no single step stands alone, and the real value is seeing the whole path at once.

When that's true — a career-change journey, a legal process, a health protocol — the map itself is the value: "Here's the complete path. Now you know where you are and where you're going."

Finding Your Hot Step

Here's the good news: you already picked it.

When you built your Magic Formula™, its final step had you flag your hot step — the single hottest ingredient of your system, the seed of this exact tool. So this isn't a hunt. It's a confirmation.

But it's worth pressure-testing, because not every step makes a good magnet. Some are fire. Some are homework.

"Set your goals" is necessary — and nobody on earth opts in for it. "The One-Page Sales Script" makes people lean in.

The five things a strong hot step needs

Specific. A tangible deliverable, not a concept. "The Pipeline Map," not "Understand your market."

Curiosity-creating. The name makes them think "What's that?" and want it.

Quick win. They can use it in one sitting — think 15 to 30 minutes — and get a real result.

Incomplete. It solves one problem and creates desire for the rest of your system.

Not boring. Skip the foundational homework steps. Pick the bright, shiny, valuable tool.

Where hot steps live

The best ones usually come from Stage 1 or early Stage 2 of your Magic Formula™ — the foundational tool that creates the first "aha" and makes them want the rest.

The worst ones are the true homework steps: goal-setting, avatar worksheets, mindset assessments. Necessary, but nobody raises their hand for them.

✓ Hot step

"The 7-Touch DM Sequence" — a specific, nameable tool that promises a result you can picture.

✕ Cold step

"Define your ideal client" — foundational homework. Important, but it makes people yawn, not opt in.

The Formats

Not everything can be an Event Magnet™. There's a clear line between a tool and a content piece.

Approved — tangible objects people USE

These deliver a result in one sitting: a one-page cheat sheet, a template (fill-in-the-blank), a checklist, a script (word-for-word), a calculator or scorecard, a one-page map, an audit or diagnostic, or a swipe file.

Every one of them is visual. You can hold it up, show it in an ad, and let people SEE it before they opt in.

Rejected — content dressed up as a tool

These teach concepts instead of delivering a result: guides, reports and white papers, ebooks, multi-page educational PDFs, blog posts with a cover, "free trainings," free calls, and full courses or challenges.

If you catch yourself building a "guide," stop and ask: what's the one TOOL inside it someone could USE in one sitting? That tool is your Event Magnet™. The rest is teaching for later.

▲ The one-sitting test

If your avatar can't USE it and walk away with a tangible result in one sitting — think 15 to 30 minutes — it's too big.

There's no 90-day Event Magnet™. Scope it down until it's one small, complete win.

The Title Is the Ad

The title is the most important words on the whole thing — because it works as the headline for your ads. That's literally what it is.

Weak title, no opt-ins. So you write it in the same format as your $Million Promise™: name the object, promise a specific result, put a clock on it, and — if it fits — name the thing they get to skip.

[Object Name] — [Specific Result] in [Timeline] — without [Obstacle]
✓ Titles that work

"The One-Page Enrollment Script — close your next client without a sales pitch." · "The Pipeline Map — find 40+ warm leads already in your network in 48 hours." · "The Profit Predictor — know what you'll make before you spend a dime."

✕ Titles that fail

"Why Your Pipeline Is Inconsistent" — a blog title, no object, no result. · "The B2B Visibility Blueprint" — vague; what IS it? · "The Complete Guide to Getting Clients" — too big, not an object.

Each strong title names a specific object, promises a specific result, and puts a timeline or effort marker on it.

If your title reads like something you'd click on in a paid ad, it's working. If it reads like a blog post, rebuild it.

The 6-Point Check

Your Event Magnet™ isn't scored out of 25 — and that's on purpose.

The hot step it's built from was already scored and locked in your Magic Formula™ (18+/25). The promise that shapes its title was already scored and locked. So the control here is a simple pass/fail gate: six checks, and all six have to pass.

This is the difference between a magnet that pulls and a file nobody downloads. If one check fails, you fix it before you finalize — that's a lot cheaper than fixing it after you've run ads to it.

#CheckPasses when…
1On-SystemIt's ONE hot step from your locked Magic Formula™ — not off-system, not invented, not a mash-up.
2VisualYou can show it on screen and it opens a curiosity loop.
3A Tool, Not a TeachIt's a tangible object they USE — not a guide, report, or ebook.
4Quick WinUsable in one sitting (~15–30 min); one small problem solved fast.
5Incomplete by DesignIt solves one piece and creates desire for the full Magic Formula™.
6Leads to the EventA clear CTA to register for your Money Magnet™ event — never a "free call."

All six green means it's ready to spec out and deploy. Any red means name the gap, fix that one element, and re-check.

If you chose the Full System Reveal, Checks 1 and 5 are read against the whole system — it's your real, locked Magic Formula™, and the map shows the path while the how-to still lives in your paid program. It still has to pass the other four.

The CTA: Point Them to the Event

Every Event Magnet™ ends with one clear next step — and that step is always the same: register for your event.

That's its whole job. It fills your Money Magnet™ events, the rooms where you make your Red Diamond Offer™.

So the CTA points to ONE thing — your event — and the framing matches your phase and your close from the Money Model.

By phase

In Launch and Execute, the CTA registers people for your free event or masterclass: "Come to [event] and I'll walk you through the whole system."

In Growth, it registers them for your paid event or books your paid strategy session — the magnet warms them up to pay a small ticket.

In Mastery, it registers them for your premium multi-day event.

⚠ Never send them to a "free call"

"Book a free call," "schedule a chat," "learn more" — these leak the momentum you just built.

Free calls attract tire-kickers and put the work back on you. Your event is where the offer gets made, at scale. Point the CTA there.

Pair it — never hand it over cold

Don't just drop the file and vanish. The moment someone opts in is your one shot at a first impression.

Pair your Event Magnet™ with a short welcome — a quick video or note that shows them how to use the tool and invites them to your event.

The full show-up, follow-up, and nurture automation is your Genie X Converter™ (Step 6), built later. For now, just plan a simple welcome so the tool never lands cold.

Use the visual everywhere

Your Event Magnet™ isn't only a download — it's your best marketing asset.

Show it in your posts and content, put it on screen as the creative in your ads, and hold it up live at your events. Show your Magic Formula™ visual right alongside it, and you get two curiosity loops in every piece: the one small tool, and the whole system it's a piece of.

Common Mistakes & Fixes

Almost every weak Event Magnet™ fails in one of the ways below.

For each, here's what it looks like, why it happens, why it quietly costs you, and exactly how to fix it.

1. A "guide" instead of a tool

What it looks like: "The Complete Guide to X" — pages of teaching with a cover.

Why it happens: Teaching everything feels generous, and it's easier than the discipline of picking one tool.

Why it costs you: Guides teach concepts; they don't hand over a result. People download them and never use them — so they never feel the quick win that makes them want more.

The fix: pull the one TOOL out of the guide. "The Complete Guide to LinkedIn" → "The 7-Touch DM Sequence." One thing they USE, not a stack they read.

2. A content-style title

What it looks like: "Why Your Pipeline Is Inconsistent" or "5 Mistakes Coaches Make."

Why it happens: Those are the headlines we see on blogs all day, so they feel natural.

Why it costs you: A content title names no object and promises no result — there's nothing to WANT, so nobody opts in.

The fix: name the object and promise a result, in promise format. "The Pipeline Map — 40+ warm leads already in your network, in 48 hours."

3. Too big / too complete

What it looks like: a magnet that tries to deliver the whole transformation for free.

Why it happens: Fear that a small thing won't be "enough" to earn the opt-in.

Why it costs you: Too much, too soon overwhelms — and if you give away the whole system, there's no reason to buy it. It's proposing marriage on the first date.

The fix: shrink it to ONE small, complete win. The smaller the problem it solves, the better it pulls. Incomplete is the point.

4. A boring hot step

What it looks like: "Define Your Ideal Client" or "Set Your Goals" as the magnet.

Why it happens: These come first in the system, so they feel like the natural starting point.

Why it costs you: Foundational homework is necessary but nobody opts in for it. First in the system rarely means hottest.

The fix: pick the bright, shiny tool — usually in Stage 1 or early Stage 2 — that creates curiosity, not the step that happens to come first.

5. A "free call" CTA

What it looks like: the magnet ends with "Book a free call" or "Learn more."

Why it happens: It's the default CTA everyone copies, and it feels like the obvious ask.

Why it costs you: Free calls attract tire-kickers and put all the work back on you, one conversation at a time. It leaks the momentum the magnet just built.

The fix: point the CTA to your event — the room where you make your offer at scale. Match it to your phase and your close.

6. Not visual — and hidden

What it looks like: a text-heavy PDF, and a beautiful magnet buried behind a landing page nobody ever sees.

Why it happens: We think of the magnet as a download, not as the marketing itself.

Why it costs you: If it isn't visual, it can't open a loop. And if you never SHOW it — in ads, videos, posts — the whole point is wasted.

The fix: make it something you can hold up and point at, then show it everywhere. The visual IS the ad.
