What Is Cash Flow Max™?

Cash Flow Max™ is the tool that builds the paid add-ons inside your event funnel: your VIP upsell and your order bumps.

Your free event fills the room. Your main offer gets closed at the event.

But right in between — at the checkout — there's money most coaches leave on the table.

A VIP upgrade and a couple of small bumps turn a free or low-ticket registration into instant cash.

That cash self-liquidates your ad spend. It's how you fill the room without going broke — the whole point of your Cash Flow Engine™.

Cash Flow Max™ = a VIP upsell + order bumps — all pulled from your system

But here's the standard that makes this work: these add-ons are not junk stuffed in to bump the total.

Every one comes from YOUR Magic Formula™, genuinely helps the buyer, and carries its own promise so it actually sells. Real value AND a bigger cart. Both, or neither.

Matthew White built Coach Launch on offers structured exactly like this — $24 Million in high-ticket sales generated and $5.2 Million in recurring client results delivered — where the cash on the way in paid for the traffic that filled the room.

How It's Different From the Event Magnet™

Don't mix these two up — they sit at opposite ends of the same funnel.

The Event Magnet™ is FREE. It finds your hot step, gives you your masterclass theme and hook, and drives curiosity to get people INTO the room.

Cash Flow Max™ is the paid end. It builds the upgrade they choose at checkout and the bumps they tick on the order form.

The Event Magnet™ fills the room. Cash Flow Max™ fills the cart.

What Cash Flow Max™ is NOT

It's not your main offer — that's your Red Diamond Offer™, closed at the event with the Sniper Close™.

It's not a pile of random freebies stacked up to look valuable — every add-on is on-system and earns its place.

And it's not a discount play — you're adding value to raise the cart, not slashing prices to chase a sale.

Your Accelerator phase decides your cash-flow structure — whether you run a VIP, single or double, how many bumps, and the price band.

This isn't a rule for its own sake. The structure that prints money at Mastery drowns a Launch audience, and a Launch setup leaves Mastery cash on the table.

Each phase is also a checkpoint. You validate your numbers there — show rate, take rates, close rate — before you move up. More on that under Know Your Numbers, below.

The VIP: Sell Access, Then Stack Value

Your VIP is where most of this cash comes from. And most people build it backwards.

They lead with freebies. But the VIP isn't about the freebies — it's about ACCESS. People upgrade to get closer to you.

So you lead with the live time, then stack real value around it.

The two core components — every VIP has these

Core 1 — the Recordings / Replays. Lifetime or extended access to the event replays, so they can revisit the breakthroughs while they build. Costs you almost nothing; worth a lot to them.

Core 2 — the VIP Q&A / extra live time. Reserved VIP seats plus dedicated Q&A or extra time with you, where they get THEIR questions on THEIR business answered. This is the real reason people upgrade — and the heart of the VIP.

The standard stacker — community

Then you add community: VIP status in a group or inner circle where they build rapport, get updates, and stay connected after the event.

Rapport is the point. The people in your community are the people who buy again.

Optional stackers — only if they're real

If you genuinely have more to give, stack it: a day-after deep-dive session, a workbook or template pack, a fast-start bonus training.

But never invent value to pad the stack. A tight VIP beats a bloated one — and everything you add should still come from your Magic Formula™ or be real access you can deliver.

Single VIP or double VIP

A single VIP is one upgrade option, anchored high to low ("normally $497, today $97"). It's the cleanest, and it's right for Execute and Growth.

A double VIP is two options, and it's right for Mastery, where the audience has earned the depth and will pay for proximity.

Double VIPWhat's in it
Option A — base VIPCore 1 (replays) + Core 2 (VIP Q&A) + community
Option B — premium VIPEverything in A, PLUS a premium layer — deeper access, a coaching or working session, a hot seat, or an experience — at a higher price

The magic words on a double VIP are "everything in A, plus…" — Option B never starts from scratch, it climbs.

This is the move that separates a cart that converts from one that just sits there.

A list of components is a cost. A named upgrade that promises a result is a deal.

So the VIP — and every single bump — gets its own $Million Promise™: name the thing, promise a specific result, put a timeline or effort marker on it.

The Order Bumps

Bumps are the impulse yeses — the small tick-box add-ons on your order form, $7 to $47.

A great bump is an obvious "well, of course" — one small thing that makes the ticket work better. It's carved from your Magic Formula™, so it's on-system, not random.

Approved formats — fast to build, fast to use

These deliver a small win in one sitting: a template or swipe file (fill-in-the-blank, copy-and-use), a cheat sheet, checklist, or scorecard (one-page, quick reference), a mini-training or short recording (one focused lesson), or a tool kit, calculator, or planner (used in one sitting).

Not a bump

A full course, a big program, a "guide" or report or ebook, a live call, or anything that takes weeks to build or hours to consume. If it's heavy, it's not a bump — scope it down or keep it for the paid program.

▲ The price band

Bumps run $7 to $47. They can go higher, but that's rarely right — and never go below $7.

Under $7 the friction isn't worth it. In band, a bump is a no-brainer add that quietly lifts every order.

Real Value, Not Junk

Here's the line you don't cross: every add-on has to genuinely help the buyer get the result faster or better.

Junk stuffed in to raise the total wins one sale and loses the relationship. Real value wins the sale AND warms them for your main offer.

The buyer-first rule

The VIP's extra value must be REAL extra value — more access, more depth, more speed — not the same thing repackaged.

If a component is just filler, cut it. A shorter, honest stack outperforms a padded one every time.

Anchor the value, don't fake it

Show each asset's value — a normal price — next to its low add-on price, so the deal is obvious. That's honest anchoring, and it converts.

But the value figure has to be defensible — a real price for that asset. A fake "$5,000 value" on a one-page checklist converts once, then gets refunded and remembered.

⚠ Don't let the cart compete with the offer

Bumps make the ticket better. The VIP makes the day better. Neither one replaces your Red Diamond Offer™.

If an add-on starts to feel like it's the main event, you've gone too far — pull it back so the cart feeds the close instead of competing with it.

Common Mistakes & Fixes

Almost every weak cart fails in one of the ways below.

For each, here's what it looks like, why it happens, why it quietly costs you, and exactly how to fix it.

1. A VIP with no promise

What it looks like: "VIP access — $97," a bare list of what's included.

Why it happens: It feels obvious what VIP means, so naming it seems unnecessary.

Why it costs you: A list of components is a cost. Without a promise, there's no result to want — so the upgrade box goes unticked.

The fix: give the VIP its own $Million Promise™. "The Offer Lab VIP — walk out with a high-ticket offer you'd pay for, in one afternoon."

2. Leading with freebies instead of access

What it looks like: the VIP headline is about the workbook and the replays, not the time with you.

Why it happens: freebies feel like "more stuff," so they seem like the value.

Why it costs you: people don't upgrade for files — they upgrade for access to YOU. Bury that and the VIP feels skippable.

The fix: lead with the VIP Q&A / extra live time. Stack the freebies around it as support, not as the headline.

3. Junk in the cart

What it looks like: random bonuses and inflated "$5,000 value" tags stacked up to make the total look big.

Why it happens: the belief that a bigger number always converts better.

Why it costs you: junk wins one checkout and loses the relationship — and fake anchors get refunded and remembered. It sours the buyer right before your main offer.

The fix: cut anything that isn't real value. On-system, genuinely helpful, honestly anchored. A tight cart beats a padded one.

4. A bump that's really a course

What it looks like: a "bump" that's a full mini-program or a big multi-module thing at $47.

Why it happens: wanting the bump to feel worth it, so it keeps growing.

Why it costs you: heavy bumps never get built, and if they do, they never get consumed — so the buyer feels behind, not helped.

The fix: shrink it to one small, fast win — a template, a cheat sheet, one focused mini-training. Keep the depth for the paid program.

5. Ignoring the phase

What it looks like: a brand-new Launch coach running a 2-option VIP and two bumps — or a Mastery coach with no VIP at all.

Why it happens: copying someone else's cart without matching where you actually are.

Why it costs you: an over-built cart overwhelms a cold audience; an under-built one leaves obvious cash on the table.

The fix: match the structure to your phase. Deviate only on purpose, with a real reason.

6. Add-ons that compete with the offer

What it looks like: a VIP so loaded it feels like the main program, so nobody needs the Red Diamond Offer™.

Why it happens: the instinct to over-deliver on the cheap thing.

Why it costs you: if the cart gives away the transformation, there's no reason to buy the real offer. You cannibalize your own close.

The fix: keep bumps making the ticket better and the VIP making the day better. The cart warms the buyer for the offer — it never replaces it.
