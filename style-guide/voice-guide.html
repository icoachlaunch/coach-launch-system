How You Sound

The voice is raw, personal, motivational, and real. Lead with your own pain before the win — always. Talk to people like a friend who's been through it, not a corporate coach. Never sound polished. Sound human. A blend of Australian and Irish colloquial expressions makes the personality unmistakable and the warmth impossible to fake.

Raw

Unfiltered, real, no polish

"I fell into construction — a broken man..."

Vulnerable

Lead with your own struggle first

Punchy

Direct

Motivational

Signature Style Elements

These are the fingerprints that make the writing unmistakable. Never strip these out.

Ellipses…

Dramatic pauses

Slow the reader down and build tension before a reveal.

"Then ONE day after 5 long years... The madness of it all dawned on me..."

CAPS

Emotional peaks

Shout the big moment, the turning point, the core lesson.

"IF I JUST STUCK WITH IT!!!"

ONE · TWO · THREE

Numbered lessons

Structure takeaways as lessons, written in full words not digits.

"ONE: Don't look back... TWO: Show up and be accountable..."

The Doubt

Doubt before the proof

Before any win, include the moment it looked like it WASN'T going to work. That's what makes results believable.

"...I thought that was going to cost me money. However..."

Mentor Credit

Credit the people who helped

It makes you real and relatable.

"Then a friend and mentor suggested I charged for my sales calls..."

The Story Arc

Almost every post follows this arc. Use it as the default template.

#SectionWhat goes here
1The HookA bold, personal opening that stops the scroll. Often vulnerability.
2The StruggleThe pain, the problem, the grind. Name it specifically — don't generalise.
3The Doubt MomentThe moment you nearly quit or didn't believe it would work. Crucial.
4The ShiftSomething changed — a mentor, an experiment, a decision. Specific details.
5The ResultThe win — with a real number where possible. Never vague.
6The LessonNumbered takeaways (ONE, TWO, THREE) the reader can apply.
7The EncouragementLift the reader up. Speak directly to them. Tell them they can do it.
8The CTAOptional soft call to action. Ask a question or invite engagement.
9Sign-OffPeace, joy and happiness to all of you 💖

Words You Avoid

✕ Kill these

Corporate language: leverage, synergy, optimise, utilise.
Passive voice: "It was decided" — always say who did it.
Long paragraphs: break anything over 3 sentences into shorter chunks.
Generic openers: "In today's world..." / "Have you ever wondered..."
Over-polished grammar that removes personality.

Grammar & Formatting Rules

Keep these — they are the voice.

  • Sentence fragments are fine. They create rhythm.
  • Contractions without apostrophes: "cant", "dont", "wont" — conversational, raw.
  • Start sentences with And, But, Or — adds punch.
  • Trailing ellipses... to slow the reader and create suspense.
  • Run-on sentences when building emotional momentum.
  • Repetition for emphasis: "Same conversation, different person, same result".
  • No back-to-back repetition of the same idea — say it once and move on.

Gold Standard Sample

This is what all of the above sounds like when it comes together.

I discovered the hard way... FREE was killing my business.

Look, when I first started in real estate coaching and consulting, I was running loads of free discovery calls. And I mean loads... My ads were killer and my pipeline was jammers.

But here's the thing that was driving me absolutely nuts... many people weren't showing up ready to buy. It was frustrating as hell.

Then one day a friend and mentor suggested I charged for my sales calls. At first I thought he was bonkers, but he said just try it for one week.

OMG, the conversion rates went through the roof. Everything changed.

Why? Because people who pay... they pay attention.

Free attracts tire-kickers. Paid attracts buyers.

Make the stand. Protect your time. Charge what you're worth.

Tone by Context

Same voice everywhere — the dial just moves.

ContextHow the dial moves
Social postsMaximum personality. Full story arc. Ellipses, CAPS, fragments. Most casual and vulnerable.
EmailsConversational and direct. Same warmth, slightly tighter. Still punchy, never corporate.
Sales & landing pagesMore direct and result-focused, but still personal. Lead with the audience's pain, not credentials.
Scripts (webinar/masterclass)High energy, motivational, story-driven. Numbered lessons work well. Speak TO one person even in a room of hundreds.
Community / coaching postsMost empathetic. Vulnerability dialled up. Encouragement at the front, not just the end.
